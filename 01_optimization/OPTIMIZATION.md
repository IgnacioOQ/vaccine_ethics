# Strand 01 — Optimization

## Question

Across the simulation's parameter space (death probability, vaccine
effectiveness, viral-age effect), where does *"Vaccinate All"* produce **more**
harm than *"Vaccinate Vulnerable Only"*? Bayesian optimization is used to search
for and characterize those harmful-vaccination regions efficiently.

## Notebook

- `Run_Simulations_Final.ipynb` — consolidated optimization runner with seeding
  for reproducibility. Runs the simulation across sampled parameters, computes
  the vax-all vs vax-vulnerable harm deltas, and renders 3D region plots.

## Inputs / outputs

- Engine: `src/simulation_class.py`, `src/agent_class.py`, `src/imports.py`.
- Writes results and figures to `../results/` (via `dump_path = "../results/"`).
- Produces `vax_hurts_3d_plot.png` and `interactive_vax_hurts_plot.html`
  (see `results/MANIFEST.md`).

## Notes

This notebook runs a long Bayesian-optimization sweep and was originally
authored in Google Colab; the clone/Drive-mount cells have been replaced with a
portable local setup. The CSV-reload cell expects the per-run output CSVs
(`vax_hurts_region_seeding.csv`, `bo_results_fixed_params_seeding.csv`) to have
been generated by a prior full run — regenerate them by executing the sweep.
